Hellen Keller

The first deaf-blind person to earn a Bachelor of Arts degree.

Here is a time line of Hellen Keller's life:

  • 1880 - Born in Tuscumbia, Alabama.
  • 1882 - In February, Helen contracts scarlet fever or meningitis and becomes deaf and blind at the age of 19 months.
  • 1886 - Meets Alexander Graham Bell in July
  • 1887 - On March 3, Anne Sullivan, a graduate of Perkins School for the Blind who is visually impaired, arrives in Tuscumbia, Alabama, to begin teaching Helen.
  • 1888 - Started attending the Perkins Institute for the Blind.
  • 1894 - Moved to New York to attend the Wright-Humason School for the Deaf.
  • 1895 - Meets Mark Twain for the first time; they remain friends for the rest of Twain's life.
  • 1900 - Begins her studies at Radcliffe College (now part of Harvard University) in September.
  • 1903 - The Story of My Life, Keller's autobiography and her first book, is published.
  • 1904 - Graduates cum laude from Radcliffe, the first person who is deafblind to earn a college degree.
  • 1924 - Begins life-long work as the public spokesperson for the American Foundation for the Blind.
  • 1960 - Meets with President John F. Kennedy, the tenth and last United States President she met.
  • 1964 - Awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Lyndon B. Johnson. This is the highest civilian honor bestowed by the United States.
  • 1965 - In an election of visitors to the New York World's Fair, Helen Keller is one of the twenty inductees into the Women's Hall of Fame, tying with Eleanor Roosevelt for the most votes.
  • 1968 - On June 1, Helen dies peacefully a few weeks before her 88th birthday at Arcan Ridge, her beloved home in Connecticut.
  • 1999 - More than 30 years after her death, Helen Keller regularly appears on lists of the world's most influential and inspirational people. In 1999 she is on the Time Magazine 100 list of the most important figures of the 20th century.
